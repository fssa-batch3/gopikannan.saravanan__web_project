Story
#Pedals4Cuase - It is the initiative by Saiprasad Shelke and Suyash Padekar to support social causes through fundraising cycling expeditions. In 2021, they completed the Mumbai to Goa cycle ride and raised 1.39L to support Bleetech which creates accessible content for students with deafness. This year they are supporting NAWPC( National Association for the Welfare of the Physically Challenged) based out of Pune which provides free of cost training, accommodation and scholarship to physically challenged students.

Background of the organization
Even today, the attitude of parents in rural areas towards children with blindness is negative. What is the use of educating such children? That is the question of parents. Mr. Rahul Deshmukh decided to work tirelessly for the future of these students and only with the help of philanthropists and organizations in the society it has been going on uninterruptedly for last 22 years.

About the organization
Mr. Rahul set up the National Association for the Welfare of the Physically Challenged (NAWPC) to help students become self-reliant and mainstream society by overcoming the limitations of blindness] with the help of assistive technology. Through the institute, students are imparted the necessary technical and vocational education and training along with college education so that students with blindness and who are underprivileged can pursue higher education, career to their full potential. In the last 22 years, a total of 1650 students with blindness and hearing-impaired students who were unprivileged enrolled in the institute have successfully graduated from the institute with proper education and training. Today, all these students are living a good life on their own and are achieving great success in various fields.

What it does ?
The education of these students is borne by the institution. Parents send their children to the institute only if these students are given free admission. Recognizing that these children can learn and become independent as well, the organization do not charge anyone. Students do not have to pay for admission. Students will be given the education they want. The only condition is that the students should participate wholeheartedly in the learning process. Students and their parents are interviewed in detail at the time of admission. Interviews take into account the child's interest in the field and similar technical and vocational education is imparted to the students along with the college education.
  • The institute has the first computer training facility in Maharashtra for boys and girls with visual impairment.
  • It runs a 'Digital Library' with all the facilities.
  • A special hostel for students is run by the organization. The hostel offers free milk, snacks, and two meals a day.


At NAWPC, the projects undertaken are being carried out with the help of donations given by generous people in society. All the funds raised would be utilized towards the administration of ongoing Projects, supporting the education of the Physically challenged students and giving scholarships for Physically challenged students.
