Story
As the country heals, it still feels like we're lagging where hunger still remains an urgency even if the pandemic is not. If it was bad, it's becoming even worse. The people have suffered for nearly 2 years now, and very soon, we will reach to point of HUNGER CRISIS; they will die of HUNGER before the virus hits them. Who are we? Feeding From Far is an initiative that has been feeding the poor and unemployed who are struggling to feed themselves during the lockdown. We managed to distribute over 30 lakh meals to the needy since the first lockdown hit through cooked meals and ration kits.

Who do we help?
Since the first lockdown, we have been taking care of several families in the largest & oldest waste dumping ground and slum in Mumbai - Govandi along with several other areas of Maharashtra like Machhimar Nagar in South Mumbai, Dharavi, Nallasopara, Mulund, and Chiplun in Ratnagiri. In these regions reside, migrants, daily wage workers, rickshaw drivers, rag pickers etc. - who were unemployed and struggling to afford basic food.
We've expanded our efforts whenever there was a necessity and the need was at its peak.
In July 2021, when Chiplun got devastated due to massive floods, our community leaders came forward and pledged to help all of those who were bit badly and lost their livelihoods.

What do we do?
To ensure the food does not perish, Feeding From Far have been distributing ration kits that last a family of 5 for an entire week. The ration kit consists of:
1) Rice 7 kg
2) Wheat 7 kg
3) Chana dal 1.5 kg
4) Sugar 1 kg
5) Oil 1 Ltr.
6) Salt 1 kg
7) Turmeric powder 150 gms.
8) Red chilli powder 150 gms

Our aim is to feed as many families through our ration kits, every week.
The packing and distribution of kits have started in new locations like Machhimar Nagar, a quaint fishing village, and Dharavi â€“ one of the biggest slum areas in Mumbai. We aim to extend our work to every location where hunger remains a concern due to the loss of livelihoods following the pandemic. Our community leaders and on-ground volunteers like Ruksana and Roshan have started the work with full zeal and enthusiasm. We have already fed 49,000 meals to 700 families hit by unemployment due to Covid.
Even as the lockdown is being lifted across the world, the issue of hunger still looms around. Please keep supporting us, your little help can bring them much closer to their distant dreams.
